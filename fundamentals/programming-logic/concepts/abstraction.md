## üá∫üá∏ Abstraction (English)

### Definition
Abstraction is the process of identifying the essential aspects of a
computational problem while omitting irrelevant details at a given level of
analysis. It enables complex systems to be understood, designed, and
implemented in a structured and incremental manner.

In programming, abstraction means focusing on **what needs to be done**, rather
than **how it will be done**, especially during early stages of development.

### Role in Programming Logic
Abstraction is a core pillar of programming logic because it enables:
- Decomposition of complex problems into smaller parts
- Separation between idea, logic, and implementation
- Construction of solutions independent of specific languages or technologies

Without abstraction, developers are forced to handle too many details at once,
increasing cognitive load and the likelihood of errors.

### Abstraction in Backend Development
In backend systems, abstraction is fundamental for:
- Separating business rules from technical concerns
- Creating reusable functions and modules
- Reducing coupling between system components

Layers such as services, use cases, and domain models are direct applications of
the abstraction principle.

### Benefits
- Reduced complexity
- Improved code readability
- Easier maintenance
- Better testability and system evolution
